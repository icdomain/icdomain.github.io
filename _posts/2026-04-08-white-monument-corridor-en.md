A corridor through which great pale rectangular blocks stand in irregular ranks: this is the White Monument Corridor.

## On the Relics

Each monument bears bands of color and rows of numerals incised across its surface. Among archaeologists, these are presumed to constitute an ancient system of marks, or perhaps a notation of some kind. Comparable relics are scattered throughout the Independent Compute Domain, and are believed to perform functions sustaining the realm itself.

The monuments of the Corridor, however, exhibit no observable response at the present time. The prevailing view holds that they have either fallen into disrepair or ceased to function entirely.

## The "Silence-as-Function" Hypothesis

A minority of researchers have advanced an alternative reading: that **this very silence is the function of the Corridor**. On this view, the role of the monuments is not to produce or transmit, but to suppress, to restrain, to limit a flow. The site does nothing, and that is precisely what it does.

The hypothesis remains poorly evidenced. Further archaeological discovery is awaited.

## A Recommendation

The Corridor offers no spectacle. It is among the quietest places in the ICD, and is recommended to the contemplative visitor as somewhere one goes to listen to silence.
