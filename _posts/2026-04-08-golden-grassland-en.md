A spike-bearing plain that stretches across the northern continent: this is the Golden Grassland.

## History

The site was for centuries a forbidden domain under the management of the royal house. Entry was prohibited, and even aerial observation was severely restricted. Following independence, a limited portion of the grassland was opened to visitors.

## Landscape

Across the surface stand countless golden spikes, arrayed without motion in a formation resembling tall grass. The alignment is too precise to be natural. The closer one approaches, the stronger the sense becomes that one is standing not upon a plain at all, but upon the surface of an immense constructed object.

At dusk, the spikes return the low light in long ranks, and the plain takes on the appearance of a silent golden city.

## The Legend of the Gateway Below

It is a persistent claim within archaeological circles that **beneath the Grassland lies an ancient gateway leading directly to the central core of the Independent Compute Domain**. The royal house has, however, denied the existence of any such structure to the present day, and excavation requests are almost never approved.

Whether the legend holds any truth remains uncertain. The visitor will nonetheless find it difficult to walk the plain without imagining the vast structure that may sleep beneath their feet.

## Access

Prior application is required for entry. The number of permitted visitors per day is strictly limited.
